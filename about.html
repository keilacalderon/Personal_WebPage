Inspired by Magic
Keila is intrigued by design, traveling, music, strategy, fabulous food, and notable conversations.


Keila came to USCB purse her dream to become a programmer. After previously working at Accenture providing maintance to the Disney websites and mobile apps she realized she knew what it was she wanted to study and enrolled in the University's Computational Science program.


As she developed her skills, her passion for programming and development became to grown. Though she was faily new to computational sceince, the subject matter gradually grew on her. The realization of creating things from catch as a programmer fascinated her. She loved having the ability to answer her own questions and solving her own problems.


Keila had gained a new set of tools which shaped her world.


By the end of her first year, there was no question what her career would be. The experience from her school projects range from creating a video game, a database, and websites for non-profits have become pivotal experiences to her development.


What will Keila do with a degree in computational science? "I'm not sure, but that's the beauty about this major- the possibilities are endless. The world relies on technology and software, and having these skills is going to open doors for me, whatever doors those might be."
